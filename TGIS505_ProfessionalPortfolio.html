#Map of Tracks of Typhoon #ArcGIS

Cartographer's Statement

Choi-Wan Typhoon is 2021's fourth Northwest Pacific named storm, formed on May 29 (UTC: -8 hour, Seattle Time Zone) in the waters east of the Philippines and this map's production time is on June 3 18:00. The source of the data came from Open Weather Data.

This map simply referred to the weather forecast map structure and adding the point of the important cities and the name of the countries, sea, cities. Thus, to make the annotation clearly on the map, the color of the based map is dull, the ocean is blue-gray and the continent is beige.

To help the audience clearly identify the change of the strength of the typhoon, the track and time spot used different colors to represent each grade of the typhoon. Moreover, the solid line means the past track and the dotted line means the predicted track. Therefore, by this map, the audience can understand Choi-Wan Typhoon was formed as a tropical depression. After gradually sucking up heated water from the surface of an ocean, it transformed into a tropical storm. Then passed through the Philippines and causing heavy rain. According to the prediction, it will turn into a tropical depression in the southeast of the island of Taiwan and move toward the direction of Japan.
